Ford GT40 '66

This car was the first American car to have won the 24 Hours of Le Mans in 1966 which was the 34th Grand Prix of Endurance.

Porsche Carrera GT

Last of its kind. The Porsche Carrera GT is the last car with a manual transmission, that has been properly designed for track. Just under thirteen thousand units have been made for the entire world.

Ferrari 250GT Berlinetta SWB

Probably one of Ferrari's most beautiful designs, the 250GT Berlinetta dominated the 1960 24 Hours of Le Mans by taking the firs 4 positions.

McLaren F1 GTR LP

It probably has the best production car engine. First intended as a flawless road car, later developed as a racing car and won the 1995 Le Mans in extreme conditions.

Pagani Zonda Barchetta

With a price tag of $23.65 million The Barchetta is the most expensive car on the market. Completely hand-built and only 3 of these cars will ever be made.

Lamborghini Sesto Elemento

Too fast to be street legal. Lamborghini built only 20 Elementos and they cost each $2.76 million.

Ferrari LaFerrari

The LaFerrari is the most extreme road-going Ferrari ever made. LaFerrari is the last Ferrari model with a mid-mounted 12-cylinder engine. It has a cost of $2.2 million.

McLaren P1 GTR

The McLaren P1 GTR is designed exclusively for track use and a total of 58 P1 GTR's were made. It's price tag was $2.6 million when new.

Koenigsegg One:1

Only 7 examples of this car were ever made. The One:1 name comes from its amazing horsepower to kilogram ratio. It had a price of $2.85 million, but now it can reach as high as $7.4 million.

Lamborghini Veneno

The Veneno was developed to celebrate Lamborgini's 50th anniversary. With only 4 units made and a price tag of $4.5 million.
